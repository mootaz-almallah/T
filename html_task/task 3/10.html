Quartz is a common mineral made of silicon dioxide (SiO₂) and is recognized for its hard, crystalline structure. As one of Earth's most abundant minerals, it appears in various rock types, including igneous, metamorphic, and sedimentary. Quartz exists in multiple colors and forms, with well-known varieties like amethyst, citrine, and rose quartz frequently used in jewelry and decorations. Thanks to its piezoelectric properties, quartz plays a crucial role in the electronics industry, helping to regulate frequencies in clocks, radios, and other devices. With a Mohs hardness of 7, quartz is highly durable and resistant to scratches, making it ideal for industrial and artistic purposes.
